Want to Win a Hackathon? Build the Right Team First!🔥 Building your hackathon team is your foundation for winning the hackathon. 1. The vibe should match: You should enjoy working with your teammates. If the vibe doesn’t match, you won’t have fun coding together, and if it’s not fun, then productivity will drop. Without that, the creativity required for building will fail. 2. Find a balanced team: The skills should complement each other. For example, in my team, there’s one frontend guy, one backend guy, one app developer, and one full stack dev—that’s balance for me. 3. Teammates should be grinders: It shouldn’t be that you assign a task to a teammate to build a community forum feature, and after 5 hours, they come back saying they failed. If Sharvin Gavad says he will build a booking management system with AI, I consider it done. Because I know he’ll find a way to build it, even if he gets stuck. If someone isn’t building their feature, the task allocation gets messed up, and someone else will have to pick it up, costing time and other features. In my case, it’s always been me and Sharvin, with the other two team members changing—maybe Soham Parab and Slayde Sequeira for app dev (crazy app developers), Zane Falcao with Flask/ML, Chhand Chaughule with Django backend skills, and Myron Dabreo / Neston Cabral with full stack skills and awesome static pages. 4. Never let egoistic people into your team: No one in my team thinks they’re the GOAT or can do everything alone. Attitude is important. Hackathon duration is short, and you don’t have time for arguments or conflicts with other team members.

# No 42. Three Years in a Row Winning Hackathons 🏆 Witnessing Growth!

--

Today, I received an unexpected and thrilling email — the project I worked on last week during Microsoft’s Global Hackathon won 1st place in the Executive Challenge! 👏

This also marks my third consecutive year winning a Hackathon award , and each time, the experience feels as exciting and rewarding as the first.

## The Project: Building an AI Business Agent

This year, our team developed an AI Business Agent — a tool designed to assist businesses by automating routine tasks and providing intelligent insights. What made this Hackathon particularly fun for me was that I got to apply some of my recent skills in vibe coding , a coding approach I’ve been practicing lately.

The Hackathon’s intense and fast-paced environment forced me to learn quickly and apply concepts in real time — from fine-tuning AI prompts to rapidly building functional prototypes. The process of seeing an idea grow step-by-step into a runnable product in such a compressed timeline gave me a real sense of achievement and pride. There’s something magical about collaboration under pressure that pushes creativity and innovation to new heights.

## What Makes Hackathons So Special?

For those unfamiliar with the concept: a Hackathon (short for “hacker marathon”) is a concentrated event where teams come together to create solutions — usually around a central theme or challenge. It’s a playground for programmers, designers, strategists, and creators to brainstorm, build, and present ideas in a limited time frame — often 24 to 48 hours or up to a week.

At Microsoft, the Global Hackathon is a highlight of the year, drawing participants from all over the world. The challenge isn’t just technical but also about teamwork, rapid iteration, and storytelling — how you communicate the value of your solution to judges and peers.

## The Journey: From Pitch to Presentation

The process starts with pitching ideas . Each person or group briefly presents a concept they’re passionate about. Teams then form around shared interests and complementary skills.

The rhythm of a Hackathon is intense — imagine waking up at 7 AM to sync with teammates in Europe, working in bursts of focused energy, and feeling the momentum of collaboration as ideas evolve minute by minute. It’s exhausting, exhilarating, and deeply fulfilling all at once.

Our team took on this challenge with enthusiasm, dividing tasks based on individual strengths and constantly refining our AI agent’s capabilities. The final presentation was the moment where all the late nights and quick pivots paid off — showcasing a product we were proud of to an audience of executives and peers.

## Why I Keep Coming Back: Three Years of Growth and Friendship

This is my third year winning a Hackathon award, and each time I approach it differently — trying new themes, learning new skills, and meeting new people. These events have become more than competitions — they’re unique opportunities to connect with passionate colleagues, build friendships, and grow professionally and personally.

There’s something special about spending a week fully immersed in creativity and collaboration with a diverse group of people. It’s not just about winning; it’s about the shared journey, the laughter, the late-night brainstorming sessions, and the thrill of building something from scratch.

## What You Can Gain from Participating in Hackathons

- At Work: Many tech companies host Hackathons to foster innovation and encourage employees to think outside their usual roles. Successful projects can receive funding and be developed into real products, sometimes even making a huge impact on company strategy and direction.

- Side Projects & Learning: Outside of work, Hackathons are excellent platforms to test ideas, develop skills, and add meaningful projects to your portfolio. If you find the right team, you might even win prizes or money!

- Networking: The connections you make are invaluable. You get to work with talented people across functions and industries — sometimes those relationships lead to mentorships, collaborations, or new job opportunities.

## Do Hackathon Ideas Actually Get Built?

Not all do — it depends on factors like feasibility, company priorities, and executive support. But many iconic products have their roots in Hackathons. For example, Spotify Wrapped , a fan-favorite yearly recap feature, began as a Hackathon project.

Seeing your concept turn into a product that users love is truly a dream come true for any product person.

## Reflections and Looking Forward

Sharing this win today fills me with gratitude for the amazing teams I’ve been part of, the skills I continue to build, and the chance to work at the intersection of creativity and technology.

I’m excited for future Hackathons and opportunities to team up with curious, talented, and passionate people. There’s nothing quite like the energy of turning wild ideas into working products in a short time.

If you’re curious about joining a Hackathon, I highly recommend it — it’s a transformative experience that teaches you more than just coding or design. It teaches you how to collaborate, adapt, and innovate under pressure.

Let’s keep dreaming, building, and winning together! 🙌
